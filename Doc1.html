Music Genre

  • Pop music

    • Pop music is a genre of popular music that originated in its modern form during the mid 1950s in the United States and the United Kingdom. The terms popular music or pop music are often used interchangeably, although the former describes all music that is popular and includes many styles.

  • Classical music

    • The term classical includes composers active in instrumental music from somewhere about 1700 to 1830. Hence the list includes among the great names those of Bach, Beethoven, Clementi, Mozart, etc.

  • Hip hop music

    • Hip Hop came together in the Bronx borough of New York City. It was the early 1970s and times were tougher than ususal for the poorer parts of urban America.

  • Rock music

    • Rock and roll, also known as rock & roll, style of popular music that originated in the Unites States in the mid 1950s and that evolved by the mid 1960s into the more encompassing international style known as rock music.

  • Jazz

    • Jazz developed in the United States in the very early part of the 20th century. New Orleans, near the mouth of the Mississippi River, played a key role in this development.

  • Kpop

    • Korean popular music or K-pop, is a popular genre of music originating from South Korea.

  • Reggae

    • Since the late 1960s, reggae has been the primary popular style of music in Jamaica. It's origins reflect the cultural hybridity roots trace back to the late 1940s and 1950s when the Jamaican recording industr was in its infancy.

  • Country music

    • Country music originated in the early twentieth century among working class Americans living in the south, especially in the Appalachian Mountains.

  • Latin music

    • The origins of Latin American music can be traced back to the Spanish and Portuguese conquest of the Americans in the 16th century, when the European settlers brought their music from overseas.
